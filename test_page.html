    Wellbeing of Our Students

    A quick investigation shows just how much students are falling behind the typical health and academic standards.

    Studying

    Students study an average of 20 hours per week, or about 4 hours every weekday. This falls behind the 2:1 ratio for study hours to instruction hours as recommended by the California Learning Resource Network, which suggests a minimum of 24 hours of studying per week.

    Hours of Sleeping

    Students sleep an average of just over 7 hours a night, which meets the typical recommendation for adults according to the Mayo Clinic, which recommends 7 hours a night for adults (18+) and 9 hours for teens (13-18).

    Hours of Physical Activity

    Students on average fit in almost 3 hours of physical activity a week, exceeding the recommended 2.5 hours a week by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).

    Exam Score

    Students on average scored a 67.2% on this exam, which earns a letter grade of D+ and a GPA of 1.3, both considered failing at most universities, including here at Notre Dame.

    The Numbers

    By studying the overlap of all four characteristics together, students' struggles become much clearer. Only 16.38% of student exceed the average in all four categories (≥20 hours of studying, ≥7 hours of sleeping, ≥3 hours of physical activity, ≥67% on their exam). In fact, the 50% percentile of all students gets studies for 19 hours, sleeps for 5, engages in physical activity for 2, and scores a 64, falling behind all the standards.
