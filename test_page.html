    Wellbeing of Our Students

    A quick investigation shows just how much students are falling behind the typical health and academic standards.

    Studying

    Students study an average of 20 hours per week, or about 4 hours every weekday. This falls behind the 2:1 ratio for study hours to instruction hours as recommended by the California Learning Resource Network, which suggests a minimum of 24 hours of studying per week.

    Hours of Sleeping

    Students sleep an average of just over 7 hours a night, which meets the typical recommendation for adults according to the Mayo Clinic, which recommends 7 hours a night for adults (18+) and 9 hours for teens (13-18).

    Hours of Physical Activity

    Students on average fit in almost 3 hours of physical activity a week, exceeding the recommended 2.5 hours a week by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). This is one of the few categories in which students actually exceed the expectation.

    Exam Score

    Students on average scored a 67.2% on this exam, which earns a letter grade of D+ and a GPA of 1.3, both considered failing at most universities, including here at Notre Dame. Notably, about 25% of students scored a C- or better on this exam.

    The Numbers

    By studying the overlap of all four characteristics together, students' struggles become much clearer. Only 16.38% of student exceed the average in all four categories (≥20 hours of studying, ≥7 hours of sleeping, ≥3 hours of physical activity, ≥67% on their exam).     Keeping the body healthy

    About 25% of students met the traditional standards of health: sleeping 8 hours and exercising for at least 30 minutes a day (which totals three hours for the week, taking Sundays as a rest day). 0.48% of students exercise for an hours a day, or six hours total, as recommended by the American Heart Association for teens

    How 'average' is a C?

    Only 5.62% of students scored a C (73%) or better, the typical standard of average in the classroom. Many teachers and employers today expect students to maintain perfect grades and GPA's, but this data shows just how difficult that task is. Only 0.23% of students received an A (>93%) on the exam.

    How do students spend their time?

    The 50th percentile described above (19 hours of studying, 5 hours of sleeping, 2 hours of physical activity, and scoring a 64) totals to 56 hours per week, only about 30% of the total time student have a week. This suggests that students spend the majority of their time outside of their studies.
