    Effort and Outcomes: How Student Choices Shape Academic Performance

    This section focuses on how students’ own behaviors and decisions influence their academic results. The parallel coordinates graph highlights the relationship between hours studied, class attendance, and exam scores, illustrating how consistent effort and engagement tend to align with higher performance. Complementing this, the bar graph comparing average exam scores across different numbers of tutoring sessions shows how additional academic support can further impact outcomes. Together, these visualizations emphasize that while external factors matter, students’ personal investment—through studying, attendance, and seeking help—plays a central role in shaping their grades.

    Patterns of Effort and Performance

    This parallel coordinates graph visualizes the relationship between students’ study habits, attendance, and exam performance. By displaying hours studied, attendance levels, and exam scores along connected axes, the graph reveals common patterns in how individual behaviors align with academic outcomes. Students who study more consistently and attend class more regularly tend to trace paths toward higher exam scores, highlighting the interconnected role of effort and engagement in academic success.
