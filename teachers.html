    Teaching Quality and School Context: How Educators Shape Student Achievement

    This page examines the influence of teacher quality on student academic performance, while also accounting for differences between public and private school settings. By comparing exam scores across low, medium, and high teacher quality levels and separating these results by school type, the visualization highlights how instructional quality interacts with institutional context. The graph suggests that higher teacher quality is consistently associated with stronger student performance, and that this effect may vary between public and private schools. Together, these comparisons emphasize the central role teachers play in shaping educational outcomes, alongside the broader environments in which they teach.

    Just how bad is a bad teacher?

    The first thing the graph clearly shows is how much increasing teacher quality positively affects a student's exam scores, and thus their education. Low and even some medium quality teachers produce students below the average on their exam scores. High quality teachers, on the other hand, produce students who do significantly better.

    High Quality Public School Teachers

    For both the low and medium quality teachers, private school teachers dominate public school teachers in terms of the exam scores they produce. However, when investigating the high quality teachers, public school teachers rise above private school teachers. Because of this, the discrepancy between medium and high quality public school teachers is huge, which speaks for just how much the quality of the good teacher affects their students.

    The Importance of Significance

    Even though the bars of this graph differ at most by 1.1% (between low-quality public and high-quality public), this difference is still statistically significant because of the large size of the database. This significance means that we can take away a meaningful message about the discrepancy between the columns to as small of a level as 0.09%.
