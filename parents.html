    Home Matters: How Family Background Shapes Student Success

    This page explores the powerful ways in which a student’s home environment influences their academic experience. By examining exam scores across varying levels of parental involvement and parental education, we highlight how support and educational background at home can shape academic outcomes. We also consider how physical distance from home affects students’ day-to-day engagement, showing that students who live farther away tend to attend class less consistently. Together, these visualizations connect family influence and accessibility to school with both performance and participation, offering a more complete picture of how factors beyond the classroom contribute to student success.

    Distance from Home, Distance from Class

    This graph focuses specifically on the relationship between how far students live from home and how regularly they attend class. By comparing average attendance levels across increasing distances, the visualization illustrates a clear pattern: as distance from home increases, consistent class attendance tends to decline. This suggests that logistical challenges—such as longer commutes, transportation limitations, or reduced daily support—may create barriers to regular attendance. Highlighting this relationship emphasizes that academic engagement is influenced not only by motivation or ability, but also by the practical realities of students’ home circumstances.

    Keeping in Touch with Family
    Students whose families live farther away from their respective school, and thus feel more responsible to keep in touch with their family, attend class less often.
